lal mahal, Pune


If you are a history lover or want to explore the rich heritage of India, Pune has a lot to offer. Royal palaces, religious temples, ancient forts, and monuments will greet you at every corner of this magical city. The Queen of the Deccan, Pune, not only houses historical attractions; it also offers pleasant weather, parks, museums, and hotels.This is why today, it has become one of the popular tourist hotspots of India. The cultural capital of Maharashtra holds the valour of the rulers of the Maratha Empire. While visiting the forts and Mahals, you will be awestruck by the glory of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. One such iconic landmark in Pune is the Lal Mahal (Red Palace). Located close to Shaniwar Wada, Lal Mahal’s existence dates back to 1630 AD. The palace represents the most valiant acts of the Maratha leaders. It witnessed great battles and the excellence of Chhatrapati Shivaji. Lal Mahal is a great tourist attraction in Pune. If you’re visiting Pune, don’t forget to explore this historical monument.
